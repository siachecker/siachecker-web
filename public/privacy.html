Privacy Policy

Last updated: 17 December 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how SIA Checker (“SIA Checker”, “we”, “us”, “our”) collects, uses, and protects personal data when you access or use our service (the “Service”).

We are committed to protecting your privacy and processing personal data in accordance with applicable data protection laws, including the UK GDPR and the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

1. Who We Are

SIA Checker is an independent service providing an API for automated checks of UK Security Industry Authority (SIA) licence numbers.

Contact email: help@siachecker.co.uk

For the purposes of data protection law, SIA Checker is the data controller of the personal data described in this Policy.

2. What Data We Process

Depending on how you use the Service, we may process the following categories of personal data:

  • SIA licence numbers submitted to the API
  • Technical data, including IP addresses, request metadata, timestamps, and user-agent information
  • Contact information, such as email addresses provided for billing or support
  • Billing and transaction metadata, such as subscription status and invoices (processed by Stripe)
  • Usage data, including API usage counts and quota consumption

We do not collect passwords, dates of birth, national insurance numbers, or payment card details.

3. How We Use Personal Data

We process personal data only where necessary for legitimate purposes, including:

  • Providing and operating the Service
  • Performing licence checks requested by you
  • Enforcing rate limits, quotas, and abuse prevention
  • Billing, invoicing, and payment administration
  • Monitoring reliability, performance, and security
  • Responding to support or legal enquiries

We do not sell personal data or use it for advertising purposes.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

We process personal data under the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract – to provide the Service you request
  • Legitimate interests – to secure, operate, and improve the Service
  • Legal obligations – including tax, accounting, and regulatory requirements

Where required, we rely on your consent, which may be withdrawn at any time.

5. Data Sources

SIA licence data is obtained from the public SIA Register of Licence Holders. We do not control the content or availability of upstream public registers.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary:

  • API logs and technical data are retained for a limited period for security and troubleshooting
  • Cached licence data may be stored temporarily to improve performance
  • Billing records are retained for the period required by applicable tax and accounting laws

When data is no longer required, it is securely deleted or anonymised.

7. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We may share personal data with trusted third parties only where necessary to operate the Service, including:

  • Stripe – for payment processing and invoicing
  • Infrastructure and hosting providers – for API operation and security

These providers process data under contractual obligations consistent with data protection law.

We do not disclose personal data to third parties for marketing purposes.

8. International Transfers

Some service providers may be located outside the UK or European Economic Area. Where personal data is transferred internationally, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, such as standard contractual clauses.

9. Your Rights

Under applicable data protection laws, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of personal data
  • Object to or restrict processing
  • Request data portability
  • Lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) or your local supervisory authority

Requests can be made by contacting us at help@siachecker.co.uk.

10. Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including access controls, encryption where appropriate, and monitoring for abuse or unauthorised access.

No system is completely secure, but we take reasonable steps to protect the data we process.

11. Children’s Data

The Service is not intended for use by individuals under the age of 18, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from children.
